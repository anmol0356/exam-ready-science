        <div class="phys">Food Chain Example:
Grass ‚Üí Grasshopper ‚Üí Frog ‚Üí Snake ‚Üí Hawk
(Producer ‚Üí Primary consumer ‚Üí Secondary consumer ‚Üí Tertiary ‚Üí Apex predator)

Forest food chain:
Plants ‚Üí Deer ‚Üí Tiger</div>
        <strong>10% Energy Rule (Lindemann's Law):</strong> Only 10% of energy is transferred from one trophic level to the next. 90% is lost as heat, respiration, etc.
        <div class="phys">Example:
Plants produce 1000 J ‚Üí Grasshopper gets 100 J (10%)
‚Üí Frog gets 10 J ‚Üí Snake gets 1 J ‚Üí Hawk gets 0.1 J

This is why food chains rarely exceed 4‚Äì5 steps, and
why there are more plants than herbivores than carnivores (ecological pyramid)</div>
        <strong>Biodegradable vs Non-biodegradable:</strong>
        <table>
          <tr><th>Biodegradable</th><th>Non-biodegradable</th></tr>
          <tr><td>Broken down by microorganisms</td><td>Cannot be decomposed naturally</td></tr>

          <tr><td>Examples: vegetable peels, paper, cotton, wood, food waste</td><td>Examples: plastic, glass, metal, DDT, polythene</td></tr>
        </table>

        <div class="phys">Ozone (O‚ÇÉ) is in the stratosphere (15‚Äì35 km altitude).
It absorbs harmful UV radiation from the Sun.
Depleted by CFCs (chlorofluorocarbons) from refrigerators, ACs.
Solution: Replace CFCs with non-ozone-depleting substances.</div>
        <div class="key-note">üí° Biomagnification: Non-biodegradable chemicals (like DDT, pesticides) accumulate and increase in concentration at higher trophic levels. Apex predators get highest concentration. This is why DDT was banned. 3 R's = Reduce, Reuse, Recycle.</div>

function searchQ(val){
  const q=val.toLowerCase();
  document.querySelectorAll('.q-wrap').forEach(w=>{
    const t=w.querySelector('.q-text').textContent.toLowerCase();
    const show=!q||t.includes(q);
    w.style.display=show?'block':'none';
    if(show&&q)w.closest('.chapter').classList.add('open');
  });
}
